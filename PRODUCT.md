# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A spectrum from beginner climbers learning structured finger training to serious athletes running repeaters and max-hang protocols. Context: mid-training session, chalk on hands, phone mounted or set nearby, needing split-second reads without cognitive friction. Not sitting at a desk — physically exerting, possibly sweating.

## Product Purpose

A focused hangboard (fingerboard) interval timer for rock climbers. Provides structured workout sessions with hold-type guidance, set tracking, rest countdowns, and preset routines for common training protocols. Success means a climber completes a session without looking away from the timer or fumbling with the UI.

## Brand Personality

Focused, minimal, precise. The app has the personality of well-made gear: no noise, no ornamentation, nothing that doesn't serve training. It earns trust through reliability, not personality.

## Anti-references

Loud sports branding — heavy drop shadows, electric gradients, aggressive display type, ESPN or Nike-App energy. This app is not trying to hype you up. It times your hangs and gets out of the way.

Also avoid: sterile clinical UIs (hospital white and teal), generic fitness app gamification (streaks, badges, confetti, neon green), and SaaS dashboard layouts (card grids, hero metrics).

## Design Principles

1. **Disappear when active.** During a timer session, the UI should recede. The countdown is the product. Everything else is support.
2. **Glanceable under load.** Information hierarchy is set for split-second mid-hang reads. Phase and time are primary; everything else is secondary.
3. **Touch tolerates chalk.** Large targets, clear affordances, forgiving hit areas. Hands are tired, sweaty, and chalked.
4. **Precise without clinical.** Numbers and data are the core medium — render them with craft, not spreadsheet energy.
5. **Dark is earned here.** This is a gym tool used in ambient light under effort. Dark theme is correct for this context, not a stylistic default.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast ratios as a baseline. Large touch targets throughout — minimum 48×48pt, prefer 56pt+ for primary controls. Phase transitions should support both visual and haptic feedback for users who can't watch the screen during a hang.
